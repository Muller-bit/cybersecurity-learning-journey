# Lesson — Spear Phishing vs Generic Phishing

---

## Core difference

- Generic phishing = spam. Wide net, low value, easy to spot.
- Spear phishing = surgical. One target, deep research, 
  looks completely legitimate.

## Why it destroys financial institutions

- Targets people with wire transfer authority
- Uses real names, real job titles, real urgency
- One success = millions moved instantly and irreversibly
- Finance has built-in urgency — "before market close" 
  is a weapon

## Real case — Bangladesh Bank 2016

Attackers studied internal SWIFT communication patterns 
for months. Sent perfectly crafted fake messages. 
Result: $81 million stolen in one weekend.

## DeFi angle

Ronin Network ($625M hack) started with a fake job offer PDF 
sent to one developer. One click. Malware installed. 
Keys compromised. Funds drained.

Spear phishing is the #1 way DeFi protocols are drained — 
not smart contract bugs. Human targets are easier than code.

## My analogy

Generic phishing = cold calling strangers.  
Spear phishing = a con artist who studied you 
for 3 months before knocking on your door.

## Exam answer framework (Security+)

Spear phishing is more dangerous than generic phishing because:
1. It is personalised — harder to detect
2. It targets high-value individuals with system access
3. It uses context that makes requests seem legitimate
4. The financial payoff is immediate and often irreversible
