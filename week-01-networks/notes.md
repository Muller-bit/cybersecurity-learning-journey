## What I learned

- A network is a group of interconnected devices communicating 
  across locations. Every connection is a potential attack surface.
- Every device has an IP address — a unique location identifier. 
  Attackers can fake this (IP spoofing) to impersonate trusted devices.
- Data travels in packets, not whole files. Attackers can intercept 
  packets in transit — called a Man-in-the-Middle attack. HTTPS 
  encrypts packets to prevent this.
- Networks have 7 layers (OSI model). Each layer has its own 
  vulnerabilities.

---

## The analogy that made it click

My Uber car = a node on a network. The road = the network. 
Passengers = data packets. I don't know who they are until 
they get in — that is exactly the security problem networks face.

---

## DeFi connection

When I send crypto, the transaction travels across a network of 
nodes. The blockchain is a network with built-in security — every 
transaction is cryptographically signed so it cannot be faked 
in transit. More secure than most corporate networks.
